FIG. 01 — OVERVIEW

Composite indicator modeling

Estimate Human Development Index scores from a country's raw indicators.

HDI Predictor takes life expectancy, education, and income figures and returns a modeled HDI score, the same composite the UNDP has published since 1990, recomputed from a regression trained on historical cross-country data.

Run the demo → Read the methodology
FIG. 02 — INPUTS

Three indicators, one score.

The model mirrors the UNDP's own dimensions of development: a long and healthy life, access to knowledge, and a decent standard of living.

DIMENSION 1

Health

Life expectancy at birth, in years.

DIMENSION 2

Education

Mean and expected years of schooling.

DIMENSION 3

Income

Gross national income per capita (PPP).

FIG. 03 — OUTPUT

A single dial, read at a glance.

The demo page renders the prediction as an index dial, scaled 0 to 1, alongside the conventional UNDP development tier (Low, Medium, High, Very High) for that score.
